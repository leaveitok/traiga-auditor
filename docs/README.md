**Mission in one paragraph:** TRAIGA Auditor is a GRC built *for* AI, not AI
bolted onto a GRC. Its buyers consume AI (vendor chatbots, embedded tools,
staff ChatGPT use) rather than build it. The moat is discovery-fed compliance:
an external scanner finds AI on a city's public web presence, the Sentinel
browser extension observes staff AI usage on city devices, and both feed one
registry that generates statutory evidence under Texas HB 149 / TRAIGA
(Tex. Bus. & Com. Code Ch. 552). No competitor scans, tracks the statutory
60-day cure clock operationally, or generates the § 552.103/.104/.105 legal
artifacts from live evidence.

**Statutory citations used throughout** (verified 2026-07-07 against enrolled
HB 149, capitol.texas.gov/tlodocs/89R/billtext/html/HB00149F.htm):
§ 552.051 government-agency AI disclosure duty · § 552.103(b) AG civil
investigative demand items · § 552.104 notice and 60-day cure ·
§ 552.105(c)–(e) reasonable-care presumption and testing/internal-review
defenses. Documents generated by the platform are engineering artifacts and
require counsel review — every generated document says so on its face.
